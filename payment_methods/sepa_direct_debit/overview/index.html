Overview - SEPA Direct Debit

Single Euro Payments Area

The aim of the Single Euro Payments Area (SEPA) is to create an efficient pan-European payments area for payments made in Euros. Cashless transactions will be made and received in and between the SEPA member countries regardless of their location. Existing sorting codes and account numbers will be replaced by an International Bank Account Number (IBAN) and a Bank Identifier Code (BIC).

For more details please refer to the official page of the European Commission.

Basic concepts of SEPA direct debit

SEPA direct debit is based on the following concept: both debtor and creditor must hold an account with a payment service provider located within the SEPA area. Altough the accounts may be in Euro or in any other SEPA currency, the transfer of money between the debtorÂ´s bank and the creditorÂ´s bank always takes place in Euro. For SEPA direct debit transactions, the debtor must grant a mandate to the creditor. By signing the mandate, the creditor is authorized to collect the relevant payments and to instruct the debtorÂ´s bank to pay the relevant funds.

SEPA direct debit is used for single (one-off) or reccurring direct debit collections.
