2. Selecting a QENTA solution

One of the most important issues when establishing your online shop and setting up a checkout solution is deciding on the QENTA solution that best suits your business requirements and scenarios.

In this chapter we intend to give you an overview of the different solutions we offer and make you acquainted with their most essential features:

  • QPAY Checkout Page

  • QMORE Checkout Seamless

  • QFILE Checkout Automated

  • QCALL Checkout Terminal

For the sake of completeness, it is mentioned that our QPC Payment Center is an integral part of all our QENTA solutions.

Please keep in mind that enhanced usability of the implemented solution also implies a higher integration effort, and thus a more intense occupation with PCI DSS related issues.

QPAY Checkout Page

QPAY Checkout Page is the perfect choice when it comes to minimum integration effort and extensive support of all popular payment methods while at the same time covering many business needs. During the checkout process in your online shop, your consumers are directed to a page hosted by QENTA to complete the checkout process. If the default design and layout of QPAY Checkout Page do not fit the design and layout of your online shop, you may modify the design respectively. This is done by customizing some template files in HTML and style sheets in CSS.

The actual payment process, i.e. when your consumer enters the payment details, is always the last step of the entire checkout procedure. After a consumer has successfully completed a payment in your online shop, this payment - depending on the payment method and the configuration settings made by our support teams - is only approved (authorized) and not yet deposited (captured).

In order to receive the funds from your financial service provider, a day-end closing has to be performed of all deposited payments. To prevent the expiration of uncaptured payments you have the possibility to use an automated deposit and day-end closing which is offered as an optional feature of QPAY Checkout Page and QMORE Checkout Seamless.

Please have a look at QPAY Checkout Page for more information and details.

QMORE Checkout Seamless

When using QMORE Checkout Seamless, as compared to QPAY Checkout Page, your consumer stays in your online shop when completing the checkout process and, depending on the selected payment method, enters the relevant payment data in HTML forms in your online shop.

The actual payment process, i.e. when your consumer enters the payment details, must not necessarily be the last step in the checkout process since you may freely decide at which point during the checkout process your consumer is prompted to enter the relevant payment data.

After a consumer has successfully completed a payment in your online shop, this payment - depending on the payment method and the configuration settings made by our support teams - is only approved (authorized) and not yet deposited (captured).

In order to receive the funds from your financial service provider, a day-end closing has to be performed of all deposited payments. To prevent the expiration of uncaptured payments you have the possibility to use an automated deposit and day-end closing which is offered as an optional feature of QMORE Checkout Seamless and QPAY Checkout Page.

Furthermore, you have the opportunity to include certain intermediate steps before the purchase is completed. These include the displaying of order overviews, additional consumer-tailored information on discounts and special offers, as well as promotions which are perfectly catered to your consumers' interests and shopping preferences. This way, your consumer is very likely to add further items to the shopping cart which is an effective method of increasing your sales.

This customized handling of the entire checkout process boosts the cross selling and upselling potential of your online shop and is the most outstanding feature of QMORE Checkout Seamless. Your consumers enjoy additional benefits, highest levels of satisfaction and approval which at the same time significantly enhances the conversion rate of your online shop.

Please find more details at QMORE Checkout Seamless.

Overview - checkout process in QPAY Checkout Page and QMORE Checkout Seamless

The following flow chart outlines the most important steps of a checkout process in an online shop using QPAY Checkout Page or QMORE Checkout Seamless and also includes the embedded payment process which is described in step 3.

  1. First, the consumer opens the shopping cart which contains the previously selected items.

  2. Then he enters his personal data and the shipping address into the provided input fields of a form.

  3. Next, the consumer selects one of the available payment methods, enters the required payment method specific data, confirms and commits the payment.

  4. Then the consumer receives information on the result of the payment (see payment process details below). After returning to the online shop, the consumer proceeds with browsing or shopping additional items in the shop which are added to a new shopping basket.

Overview of responsibilites in checkout process

The following charts shall help you to understand which steps in the checkout process are realized by the merchant and which steps are handled by QENTA.

QPAY Checkout Page

The selection of payment methods is done by the merchant or by QENTA when using payment method SELECT, i.e the consumer may select one of the activated payment methods directly within QPAY Checkout Page.

QMORE Checkout Seamless

When using QMORE Checkout Seamless, the entry of sensitive payment data is typically carried out in HTML forms provided by the merchant; the data are then directly transferred to QENTA to be stored in a secure manner. However, when using QENTA’s “PCI DSS SAQ A Compliance”-feature, the consumer enters credit card relevant data directly in a web form hosted by QENTA and is then redirected to your online shop.

Overview - payment process as seen by the consumer

From the consumer’s point of view, the payment process in QPAY Checkout Page or QMORE Checkout Seamless involves the following steps:

  1. First, the consumer selects one of the available payment methods offered in the online shop.

  2. Then he enters the payment method specific payment data or identification data such as credit card number, expiration date, username, password, etc.

  3. Afterwards the consumer commits the payment by clicking a relevant button.

  4. In the end, the consumer receives relevant information on the result of the payment.

4. Deciding on shop system

After you have gained the first insight and perhaps pre-selected a QENTA solution that best suits your requirements, you need to decide which shop system to use for your online shop.

Criteria for shop system selection

The following criteria are intended as a guideline to determine which shop system to use for your online shop:

  • What is the current business size?

  • What is the intended future business size?

  • What is your product portfolio for e-commerce?

  • Is online marketing used already?

  • Is there a Content Management System which needs to be integrated?

  • How are different departments such as sales, marketing, and IT going to be integrated?

  • What kind of tools and technologies are already being used and need to be taken into account?

  • Commercial shop system or free-of-charge shop system?

  • Does the preferred online shop provide sufficient scalability?

  • Do you need reporting and evaluation tools to handle your product and customer data?

  • What administration features such as data import, update/upgrade processes and automated accounting are supported by the online shop?

Integration into shop systems

Once you have decided on a shop system, you may integrate one of our solutions via a shop plugin. Optionally you may want to acquire a shop system with an existing QENTA integration, i.e. a shop system with pre-integration. If you decide to create your own shop system, a direct integration of one of our solutions is required.

QENTA Payment CEE offers free open source shop plugins for many popular shop systems which serve as technical connection between your shop system and QPAY Checkout Page or QMORE Checkout Seamless. 5. Clarifying PCI DSS issues

The Payment Card Industry (PCI) Data Security Standard (DSS) is a set of rules and guidelines aimed at ensuring a secure handling of sensitive payment data during online transactions.

With QENTA as PCI DSS compliant third-party payment processor, it is easier for you to comply with the relevant SAQ, SAQ A or SAQ A-EP, requested by your acquirer since no additional PCI-compliance for your online shop is required.

For more information and details on PCI DSS related issues, implications on your online business and applicable PCI DSS SAQs, we invite you to have a look at Securing your online shop.

6. Considerations on back-end operations and payment methods

Back-end operations allow you to use commands which enable various operations to be carried out regarding the orders of your consumers or the payment process itself. These operations are called by a server-to-server request from your web server to the QENTA Checkout Server. You may either call these operations automatically from the back-end of your online shop or manually from your user interface you have implemented for administration purposes in your online shop, or carry out these operations using the QPC Payment Center.

Back-end operations for QPAY Checkout Page and QMORE Checkout Seamless are subdivided into two categories:

  • Transaction-based operations*
    Transaction-based operations depend on existing orders, i.e. orders which have been completed by the consumers in your online shop and which are at your disposal for further actions.

Non-transaction-based operations
Non-transaction-based operations do not depend on existing orders. I.e. such operations are carried out independently of any existing orders.

As the availability of each back-end operation is, amongst other factors, dependant on the payment methods offered in your online shop, we recommend to first reflect on the interaction of these two factors - back-end operation and payment method. If you for instance handle subscriptions or membership fees, you might be interested in billing a fixed amount of money on a regular basis. Such back-end operations are called recurring payments and are basically supported by payment methods like credit card, PayPal, or SEPA Direct Debit.

For a basic summary of all our transaction-based back-end operations and the payment methods which support these operations visit Available operations per payment method.

Selecting payment methods for your online shop

Deciding on the adequate payment methods is a very important issue in the course of setting up an online shop since consumer acceptance and satisfaction as well as the conversion rate of your online shop are significantly influenced by the available payment methods. Studies[3] have shown that a very high percentage of consumers are very likely to cancel the checkout process if their preferred payment method is not offered by the online shop.

We recommend to place the relevant payment method icons right on your website - this is a very effective measure to increase your shop’s conversion rate. Information on the available payment methods is very important for your consumers. If the consumer is informed about the available payment methods too late, i.e. when starting the checkout process, consumers tend to cancel the whole process.

For further information and assistance please do not hesitate to contact our sales teams or support teams.

QENTA Payment CEE will gladly support you to enhance your online business and invites you to download the required payment method logos.

Selection criteria for offering the right payment method

When deciding upon the payment methods to be offered in your online shop, you should keep in mind the following considerations:

  • Which payment methods are preferred in your target countries?

  • Which payment methods are popular with your target consumers?

  • Which payment methods are popular with your target age group?

  • Which payment methods are best suited for the consumer devices used by your target consumers?

  • Which payment method is suitable to achieve high conversion rates?

  • Which payment methods are appropriate for your specific business model, e.g. retail or digital goods?

  • Do your preferred payment methods support the back-end operations you need?

  • What kind of products do you sell online? E.g. cards with 3-D Secure are advisable for digital goods.

  • Do you expect a high or low amount of fraudulent transactions?

Consumer preferences vs business model requirements

When it comes to deciding between the payment methods preferred by your consumers and the payment methods that best suit your business needs, some difficulties might arise in aligning the interests of the parties involved. E.g. while consumers prefer invoice, PayPal and SEPA Direct Debit as payment methods, your might prefer payment in advance because of the maximum protection against payment default, like eps Online-Überweisung, giropay and Sofort.

It is thus advisable to offer in your online shop a payment method mix which is aimed both at fulfilling your business model requirements and suiting the preferences of your target consumers. Online shops offer on average between five and seven different payment methods in order to meet their consumers' needs and to keep conversion rates high.

Please contact our sales teams for more information and expert advice on the best payment method mix suited for your requirements.

8. Closing contracts

As a next step you need to close the necessary contracts with QENTA Payment CEE and your financial service providers in order to go live.

8.1. Contract with QENTA Payment CEE

From a technical point of view, every payment request needs to be processed by QENTA Payment CEE as your payment service provider for which you need to conclude a contract for the technical implementation of your desired solution, required payment methods, additional features and transaction packages which are specifically tailored to your business needs. Based on the details of your contract, our support team will then set up your merchant account configuration matched to your requirements.

For detailed information regarding the contractual terms, please contact our sales teams.

8.2. Contract with financial service provider

During the checkout process, QENTA Payment CEE transmits the relevant payment information to the respective financial service provider (FSP), the payment is approved or denied and corresponding information is sent back to QENTA Payment CEE. The processed amount is then transferred to your merchant account by the financial service provider.

Typical financial service providers are credit card companies, banks and banking systems. Their field of activities include accepting money transfer requests, approval or denial of transactions and processing of payments.

From a merchant’s perspective, an acceptance contract needs to be concluded with the relevant financial service provider for any payment method offered in the online shop, the so called processing model. An acceptance contract contains information on the account to which the financial service provider will transfer the processed amounts, rules and regulations as well as the payment details like time limits and disagios.

We also provide a collecting model for which only one contract needs to be concluded with QENTA Bank which acts as your acquirer and consolidates all payment flows to one bank account. I.e. this model allows you to collect payments from various payment methods to one single bank account.

After having signed a contract with the financial service provider(s), you or the financial service provider need to forward to us the relevant contract details. Our support team then includes these details into your merchant account configuration and performs tests to verify the proper processing of payments. Only after the proper processing of payments is ensured, our support team forwards to you by fax or e-mail the respective data which are necessary for activation as well as a summary of all payment methods that will be enabled in your online shop.

The activation data include two important pieces of information:
- Data which you need to save in your integration in order to go "live". To switch from demo mode to live operation you need to remove the customerId, secret and possibly shopId which you used in demo mode and replace them with the corresponding activation data.

  1. Access data to the QPC Payment Center to view and manage all successful transactions.

Once these procedures have been performed, your consumers may successfully complete the checkout process in your online shop and, depending on the details of your acceptance contract, you will receive the corresponding funds from your financial service provider.

9. Filling out relevant SAQ

To ensure the highest level of data security, your acquirer for credit card payments will request you to fill out a specific Self-Assessment Questionnaire (SAQ) prior to concluding an acceptance contract with you. The handling and management of sensitive payment data in online shops is always a crucial issue in fulfilling the relevant SAQs and obtaining PCI-compliance. However, there is no need for your online shop to handle or store sensitive data since all necessary financial and personal data required for the checkout process are handled by QENTA.

Also, when choosing QENTA as your PCI-compliant third-party payment processor, it becomes much easier for you to comply with the relevant SAQ requested by your acquirer and no additional PCI-compliance for your online shop is required.

There are various SAQ versions available for your acquirer to select from which best suit your business profile.

For more information and details on SAQs in general and applicable SAQs for different QENTA solutions, we invite you to have a look at Securing your online shop.
